A Perspective on Market Volatility

Dear Investor,

As promised, please find attached my analysis of the current market situation. The objective of this note is to provide perspective by comparing the present volatility with several major market disruptions witnessed over the past two decades.

Historically, markets have faced multiple periods of extreme uncertainty—such as the Global Financial Crisis, India’s 2016 Demonetisation, the 2020 Global Pandemic, and the ongoing geopolitical conflicts. In each of these periods, markets initially reacted sharply to prevailing fears. However, by the time stability returned, indices were often trading significantly higher than their previous peaks.

A relevant example is the 2010–2013 period when oil prices surged to nearly $108 per barrel. Yet as stability returned, markets not only recovered the losses but continued rewarding patient investors. At present, we are again navigating uncertainty around global energy and dollar dynamics.

Scenario 1: Swift Resolution

Swift geopolitical resolution, which could lead to a sharper and more immediate market recovery as risk appetite returns.

Scenario 2: Prolonged Equilibrium

A longer adjustment period where global supply chains adapt. History suggests markets eventually discount negative narratives and move forward as fundamentals stabilize.

It is important to remain focused on long-term objectives and not allow short-term market noise to derail a well-structured investment strategy. I look forward to discussing this analysis with you in greater detail during our next interaction.
